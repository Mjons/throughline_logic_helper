# Strategic Pivot: Enterprise Model & "American Medical Blockchain Journal"

> Based on Marc's feedback call (April 18, 2026) after his conversation with Dr. Bone.
> This represents a fundamental rethink of go-to-market, positioning, and funding strategy.

---

## The Core Insight

**Current approach**: Door-to-door sales of $2,000/year licenses to 9,000 MedChi physicians. Requires hiring sales people, cold outreach, slow conversion.

**Proposed approach**: Sell ONE wholesale/enterprise contract to the state medical association. MedChi has a **$6B annual budget managed by the state**. 9,000 doctors x $2,000 = **$18M contract**. That's **0.3% of their budget**.

> _"No one's going to write a check more frivolously than a fucking government organization."_ -- Marc

The association pays for all their doctors, then we focus on **implementation and training** -- not selling. This is the Scotiabank model: 100K employees, company buys the virtual doctor program for everyone, then pushes adoption. Not "offer it and let employees pay for it."

---

## The Rebrand: "American Medical Blockchain Journal" (AMBJ)

Dr. Bone kept referring to protocols as "minted journals." Marc's insight: **that's exactly what this is -- a medical journal built on blockchain.**

### The Positioning Shift

| Before                                            | After                                                               |
| ------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| "Protocol marketplace with blockchain provenance" | "The first American medical journal built on blockchain"            |
| Physicians license proprietary protocols          | Physicians publish to a continuously-updated, peer-reviewed journal |
| Compete with ChatGPT / ambient scribes            | Compete with traditional medical journals (Harvard, NEJM, JAMA)     |
| Blockchain is a feature                           | Blockchain is invisible infrastructure -- never mentioned in sales  |

### Why This Works

1. **Familiar framing** -- Every physician understands what a medical journal is. They've been reading them their whole career. "Blockchain marketplace" requires explanation; "medical journal" doesn't.
2. **Solves the speed problem** -- Traditional journals take **17 years** from research to clinical practice. With continuous updates on-chain, that drops to **~3 years** (5x faster). That's a headline investor stat.
3. **Solves fragmentation** -- There are hundreds of medical journals. None capture individual physician-authored patient protocols as a durable, attributable legacy. When a physician retires, their protocols die with them. AMBJ is the first to preserve them.
4. **Trademarkable** -- "American Medical Blockchain Journal" or similar should be trademarked immediately. This becomes the brand, not "Delphica Marketplace."
5. **Blockchain in the background** -- Nobody needs to know it's blockchain. It's just how we organize files, track updates, and maintain open-source verifiability. This matches what we decided earlier (call it "provenance records," never say "NFT").

### Competitive Landscape Check

**"Blockchain in Healthcare Today"** (2017, Partners in Digital Health) -- the world's first peer-reviewed open access journal on distributed ledger research in healthcare. **This exists but does something different**:

- They archive **clinical trials research** -- data around DLT in healthcare
- They don't capture **physician-authored patient protocols**
- They're about academic research, not practicing physicians solidifying what works at the bedside

**What doesn't exist**: any journal that captures "individual physician-authored patient protocols as a durable, attributable legacy." That category is open. Delphica/AMBJ owns it.
